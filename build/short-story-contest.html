The Booksie 2024 Short Story Contest

Contest Closed

$500 grand prize winner. Enter your short story for exposure, fans, and a chance to win.

We're excited to announce the Booksie 2024 Short Story Competition. In partnership with WorldMaker Media, we're looking for the most interesting, original stories that will make us laugh, cry, and see the world in a slightly different way. The winner will receive the Booksie 2024 Short Story Award which comes with a cash prize, a gold trophy badge recorded on the blockchain, and the option to have the work published as a Booksie Limited Edition. Everyone wins by receiving exposure and a participation badge.

All Genres and Themes

The contest is open to all genres and themes. You can enter a previously posted story on Booksie or post a new one and enter it. 

Prizes

Grand prize winner

$500 cash award

Gold winner badge shown on Booksie and encoded on the blockchain as proof.

A writer option to receive an additional $100 contract from WorldMaker Media to publish the winner as a Booksie Limited Edition.

Two runners-up 

$100 cash

Silver winner badge shown on Booksie and encoded on the blockchain as proof.

A writer option to receive an additional $50 contract from WorldMaker Media to publish the winner as a Booksie Limited Edition.

All entrants

Receive a participation badge encoded on the blockchain as proof.

Cost

The cost to enter the contest is $6.99 per entry ($3.50 for Premium site members).

Any profit Booksie makes after setup-fees, marketing expenses, judge fees, and prize fees will be donated to the literacy non-profit Reading is Fundamental.

Frequently Asked Questions

Many of the most common questions are answered in our contest rules.

Is there a word limit on contest entries?

There is no mimimum limit. The maximum length is 5,000 words.

Can I enter as many stories as I want?

Yes, you can enter an unlimited number of stories.

Do I have to be a member of Booksie to enter?

Yes, you must be a Booksie member but membership is free. If you are not a member, you can easily become one as part of the entry process.

What are these tropies and badges encoded on the blockchain?

The blockchain represents an innovative way to certify information. By encoding trophy badges on the blockchain, winners can point to the entry as definitive proof of their achievement. For those who entered, it provides a fun credential to show participation. We will provide more information on how to use these badges.

Are you money grubbing vampires who wish to suck the precious dollars out of struggling writers with your contest fees?

Notwithstanding all of the snarky comments on Facebook, Booksie makes no money from its contests. We try to make our fees as low as possible to allow everyone to enter. We run them to publicize the site and attract users to the platform. Any profit we might make from a contest after setup and admin fees, marketing expenses, judge fees, and prize fees, will be donated to the literacy non-profit Reading is Fundamental.

Booksie 2024 Short Story Contest

Official Competition Rules and Regulations

1. OPEN ONLY TO MEMBERS OF BOOKSIE.

Not a member? Register free or use the Quick-entry process.

Members of Booksie.com may enter the Competition by:

Submitting the entry using the Contest tab.

The challenge of this competition is to post the most original, creative short story. There is no minimum length. The maximum length is 5,000 words.

Members may submit as many entries as they like. There is a fee of $6.99 per entry. This covers adminstrative costs, judging fees, marketing, and prize awards. Any profit derived from the contest fees will be donated to the literacy non-profit Reading is Fundamental.

Sponsor reserves the right to declare any entries determined to not be in accordance with the stated terms of Booksie Member Agreement ineligible for entry.

2. Winner Selection:

There will be 20 finalists who will be chosen approximately six weeks after the submission deadline. Two weeks after the finalists are announced, three winners will be chosen, a grand prize winner, and two runner-ups. Judging will be done by the Booksie and WorldMaker Media editorial staff. The editorial staff will be judging the story based on the following rubric:

40% - Originality/Creativity - is the concept unique and different from other stories out there? 

30% - Impact - does the story leave us with a feeling? Is it a story we won't soon forget?

15% - Writing quality - does the writing flow? Is it free of grammatical mistakes and typos?

15% - Commercial appeal - Will others like the story? Does it deal with a concept or subject matter that is relevant, topical, or will gain traction if published?

I would like to join in next. If chosen, I would like to suggest _your_ short story (lol) "The Secret Lives of Strangers" for inspiration (https://www.booksie.com/467912-the-secret-lives-of-strangers)

I'd like to write my entry as part of that story's miniverse. I realize this may be slightly outside the above "prompting" rules, but please accommodate as possible. As per my comment in your story's page, you already know I had intended to do a follow up piece on Secret Lives. I had a different idea for the style I’d employ. I am more into what I call "structure-less rambling" which is a style inspired on James Joyce's and Julio Cortazar's "stream of consciousness" literary styles (tho significantly less polished than theirs, lol). Anyway, regardless of style, this is a good opportunity to deliver on my intent to write on your enticing miniverse.

There is, of course, a wrinkle: Your rules say (if chosen) the next contest entry must follow my style. However, I have not published anything on Booksie, yet. Let's do this: if I'm chosen, the next entry should impersonate James Joyce's writing style in the short story "Eveline" (published in his "Dubliners" collection of shorts and easily accessible from your local library or $0.99 on Kindle). In spite of that big name, the style isn't intimidating at all. Quite the opposite, it's refreshingly simple and filled with one-liners and first person narrative.

An alternative would be for me to pick a booksie entry in my stead. But the booksie/style I'd pick in that case is a difficult style to follow. She's a brave writer, unafraid to take her story to depths I suspect not too many would follow and that courage is very much part of her style, IMO. Lastly, I should add that circumventing rules isn’t my thing (as chaos could ensue if each person picks which rules to ignore), but I see this as the best way for me to participate and keep the contest going. Let me know what you decide.
